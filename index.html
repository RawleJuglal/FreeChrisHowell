Justice for Chris

Fighting for a fair trial and freedom

What Happened?

Chris was convicted without a fair trial. New evidence and investigative misconduct have come to light, including:

  • The State failed to respond to his post-conviction filing in time, violating Oklahoma law (22 O.S. § 1089(D)(1)).
  • Chris was denied an evidentiary hearing, despite presenting newly discovered material facts (22 O.S. § 1084).
  • Evidence was withheld, testimony conflicted, and key figures failed to disclose personal involvement, violating due process and Brady/Napue standards.

Why It Matters

Chris has spent years behind bars based on a flawed process. This isn’t just about one man—it’s about ensuring our justice system follows its own rules. If this can happen to him, it can happen to anyone.
